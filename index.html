Fashion Label

Silica

silica is a womenswear label that offers handcrafted, essential pieces for everyday life. Read More

The bespoke wordmark was carefully constructed to ensure a look that’s both timeless and forward-thinking. A subtle glow was added to the serif’s edges to sharpen its form and give it a lightweight, modern, and distinct touch. The set of 3 stars symbolize the 3 atoms that make up the compound silicon dioxide.

silica launches in Spring / Summer 2018 with Wildflower, and seen here are parts of the promotional campaign leading up to its release. The project includes two advertisements, an invitation to the Paris Fashion Week debut, lookbook excerpts, and a website mockup styled to match the SS18 campaign.

Web Design

Brand New World

Brand New World is an environmental sustainability conference concept. The event’s site was built from the ground up and completed as the final project of General Assembly’s 10-week Front-End Web Development course. Visit it here. Read More

The flashy, rotating title sets the tone for BNW’s fully functioning, fully responsive digital experience. Basketball Magazine

Court Vision

Court Vision is a biannual magazine that covers basketball culture in different locations around the world. Read More

The title comes from a term used to describe a player’s ability to see and understand everything happening during a game. Court Vision takes that idea and applies it to new cities in each issue by telling the unsung stories from their streets.

Issue 1 explores Shanghai, New York City, Paris, and Mogadishu through features, interviews, and photo essays. Displayed are two spreads from an article on Somali basketball, an interview with a New York local, and the intro spread to a feature on the courts of Shanghai.

Dimensions: 17.5 x 24 cm

Art History Book

Circa 1520

Circa 1520 is an art history book that examines the Mannerist period during the later years of the Renaissance. This style took elements from earlier Italian art and expanded on them in unnatural ways. Mannerist works are notable for their contorted figures posed against tense, dramatic backdrops. Read More

This book covers the style in extensive detail, presenting historical analysis and full-color artwork renderings across eight different chapters. The classical subject matter and body text are contrasted with a sharp blue and yellow color scheme, a condensed sans-serif for detail text, and dynamic use of exposed content to highlight the art. Pictured are a number of spreads from Circa 1520.

Dimensions: 8.25 x 11.5 in

Museum Identity

Cal MoCA

Visual identity for the California Museum of Contemporary Art, a museum, cultural center, and educational institution that offers a vast collection of modern works. Read More

Located in Long Beach, CAL MoCA’s galleries and exhibitions provide its visitors with an experience that fosters creativity and makes the art of our times accessible and meaningful.

The CAL MoCA identity is anchored by a sturdy wordmark which incorporates the RYB color model used for painting. The colored divisions give the state abbreviation a recognizable feature that is as playful as it is pertinent, while also providing the general backbone for the museum's branding. Several elements are produced in sets of three in correlation to those colors. Additionally, a brushstroke texture is used on collateral to keep the logo from falling flat.

The concept includes a membership card, business cards, tickets, a guidebook featuring the museum exterior, and a series of brochures incorporating displayed paintings. A bus stop advertisement was made for an upcoming exhibit as well. Close
